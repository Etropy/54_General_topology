\begin{THM}
Let $(X_i,\vr_i)$ be a metric space for each $i=1,2,\ldots, $ where
the diameter of $X_i$ using $\vr_i$ is less than $1/i$.  Then the product topology for the space $\prod_{i=1}^\infty X_i$ is given by the metric
$$\vr(x,y)=\sum_{i=1}^\infty \frac1{2^i}\varrho_i(x_i,y_i).$$
Hence, a countable product of metrizable topological spaces is again metrizable.
\end{THM}

Since the convergence in the product topology is the pointwise convergence, the same is true for the metric space with the above metric.
