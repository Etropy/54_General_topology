cell attachment


Let X be a topological space, and let Y be the adjunction Y:=X∪φDk, where Dk is a closed k-ball (http://planetmath.org/StandardNBall) and φ:Sk-1→X is a continuous map, with Sk-1 is the (k-1)-sphere considered as the boundary of Dk. Then, we say that Y is obtained from X by the attachment of a k-cell, by the attaching map φ. The image ek of Dk in Y is called a closed k-cell, and the image e∘k of the interior

D∘:=Dk∖Sk-1

of Dk is the corresponding open k-cell.

Note that for k=0 the above definition reduces to the statement that Y is the disjoint union of X with a one-point space.

More generally, we say that Y is obtained from X by cell attachment if Y is homeomorphic to an adjunction X∪{φi}Dki, where the maps {φi} into X are defined on the boundary spheres of closed balls {Dki}.
