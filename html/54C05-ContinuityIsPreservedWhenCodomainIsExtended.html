continuity is preserved when codomain is extended


Theorem 1.

Suppose X,Y are topological space and let Z⊆Y be equipped with the subspace topology. If

f:X→Z

is continuous, then

f:X→Y

is continuous.

Proof.

Let U⊆Y be an open set. Then

f-1⁢(U) = {x∈X:f(x)∈U}
= {x∈X:f(x)∈U∩Z}
= f-1⁢(U∩Z).

Since U∩Z is open in Z, f-1⁢(U) is open in X. ∎
