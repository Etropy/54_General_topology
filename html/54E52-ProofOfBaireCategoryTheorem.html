proof of Baire category theorem


Let (X,d) be a complete metric space, and Uk a countable collection of dense, open subsets. Let x0∈X and ϵ0>0 be given. We must show that there exists a x∈⋂kUk such that

d⁢(x0,x)<ϵ0.

Since U1 is dense and open, we may choose an ϵ1>0 and an x1∈U1 such that

d⁢(x0,x1)<ϵ02,ϵ1<ϵ02,

and such that the open ball of radius ϵ1 about x1 lies entirely in U1. We then choose an ϵ2>0 and a x2∈U2 such that

d⁢(x1,x2)<ϵ12,ϵ2<ϵ12,

and such that the open ball of radius ϵ2 about x2 lies entirely in U2. We continue by induction, and construct a sequence of points xk∈Uk and positive ϵk such that

d⁢(xk-1,xk)<ϵk-12,ϵk<ϵk-12,

and such that the open ball of radius ϵk lies entirely in Uk.

By construction, for 0≤j<k we have

d⁢(xj,xk)<ϵj⁢(12+⋯+12k-j)<ϵj≤ϵ02j.

Hence the sequence xk,k=1,2,… is Cauchy, and converges by hypothesis to some x∈X. It is clear that for every k we have

d⁢(x,xk)≤ϵk.

Moreover it follows that

d⁢(x,xk)≤d⁢(x,xk+1)+d⁢(xk,xk+1)<ϵk+1+ϵk2,

and hence a fortiori

d⁢(x,xk)<ϵk

for every k. By construction then, x∈Uk for all k=1,2,…, as well. QED
