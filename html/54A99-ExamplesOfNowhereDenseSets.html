examples of nowhere dense sets


Note that ℤ is nowhere dense in ℝ under the usual topology: int⁡ℤ¯=int⁡ℤ=∅. Similarly, 1n⁢ℤ is nowhere dense for every n∈ℤ with n>0.

This result provides an alternative way to prove that ℚ is meager in ℝ under the usual topology, since ℚ=⋃n∈ℤ⁢ and ⁢n>01n⁢ℤ.
