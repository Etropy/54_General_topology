Hewitt-Marczewski-Pondiczery theorem


The Hewitt–Marczewski–Pondiczery Theorem is a result on the density of products of topological spaces. This theorem was arrived at independently by Hewitt[1], Marczewski[2] and Pondiczery[3] in the 1940s.

Theorem.

Let κ be an infinite cardinal number and S an index set of cardinality at most 2κ. If Xs (s∈S) are topological spaces with d⁢(Xs)≤κ then

d⁢(∏s∈SXs)≤κ.

The special case κ=ℵ0 says that the product of at most continuum many separable spaces is separable.
