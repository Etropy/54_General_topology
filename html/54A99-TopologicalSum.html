topological sum


Given two topological spaces X and Y, their topological sum is defined to be the set X⁢∐Y (see the entry disjoint union) equipped with the finest topology such that the inclusion maps from X and Y into X⁢∐Y are continuous. A basis for this topology consists of the union of the set of open subsets of X and the set of open subsets of Y.
