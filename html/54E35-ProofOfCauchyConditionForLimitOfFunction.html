proof of Cauchy condition for limit of function


The forward direction is . Assume that limx→x0⁡f⁢(x)=L. Then given ϵ there is a δ such that

|f⁢(u)-L|<ϵ/2⁢ when ⁢0<|u-x0|<δ.

Now for 0<|u-x0|<δ and 0<|v-x0|<δ we have

|f⁢(u)-L|<ϵ/2⁢ and ⁢|f⁢(v)-L|<ϵ/2

and so

|f⁢(u)-f⁢(v)|=|f⁢(u)-L-(f⁢(v)-L)|≤|f⁢(u)-L|+|f⁢(v)-L|<ϵ/2+ϵ/2=ϵ.

We prove the reverse by contradiction. Assume that the condition holds. Now suppose that limx→x0⁡f⁢(x) does not exist. This means that for any l and any ϵ sufficiently small then for any δ>0 there is xl such that 0<|x⁢l-x0|<δ⁢and⁢|f⁢(xl)-l|≥ϵ. For any such ϵ choose u such that 0<|u-x0|<δ and put l=f⁢(v) then substituting in the condition with u=xl we get |f⁢(xl)-l|<ϵ. A contradiction.
