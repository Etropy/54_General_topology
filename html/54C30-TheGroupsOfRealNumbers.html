Proposition 1.

The additive group of real number ⟨R,+⟩ is isomorphic to the multiplicative group of positive real numbers ⟨R+,×⟩.

Proof.

Let f⁢(x)=ex. This maps the group ⟨ℝ,+⟩ to the group ⟨ℝ+,×⟩. As f has an inverse f-1⁢(x)=ln⁡x we observe f is invertible. Furthermore, f⁢(x+y)=ex+y=ex⁢ey=f⁢(x)⁢f⁢(y) so f is a homomorphism. Thus f is an isomorphism. ∎

Corollary 2.

The multiplicative group of non-zero real number R× is isomorphic to Z2⊕⟨R,+⟩.

Use the map f:ℤ2⊕⟨ℝ,+⟩→ℝ× defined by f⁢(s,r)=(-1)s⁢er.11We write (-1)s to mean (-1)s′ for any integer s′ representative of the equivalence class of s in ℤ2. Then

so that f is a homomorphism. Furthermore, f-1⁢(r)=(sign⁡r,ln⁡|r|) is the inverse of f so that f is bijective and thus an isomorphism of groups. ∎
