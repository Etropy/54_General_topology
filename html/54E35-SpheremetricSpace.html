sphere (metric space)


The set {x∣d⁢(x,c)=r} is called the sphere of radius r with centre c. This generalizes the notion of spheres to metric spaces.

Note that the sphere in a metric space need not look like a sphere in Euclidean space. For instance, if we impose the metric d⁢(x,y)=m⁢a⁢x⁢{|x1-y1|,|x2-y2|,|x3-y3|} on ℝ3 instead of the Euclidean metric, spheres according to this metric are actually cubes! Even more bizarre situations can occur in general — a sphere might be disconnected, or it may be discrete, or it may even be an empty set.
