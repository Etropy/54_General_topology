Theorem 1.

Every totally bounded subset of a metric space is bounded.

Proof.

Let K be a totally bounded subset of a metric space. Suppose x,y∈K. We will show that there exists M>0 such that for any x,y we have d⁢(x,y)<M. From the definition of totally bounded, we can find an ε>0 and a finite subset {x1,x2⁢…,xn} of K such that K⊆⋃k=1nB⁢(xk,ε), so x∈B⁢(xi,ε),y∈B⁢(xl,ε), i,l∈{1,2,…⁢n}. So we have that

d⁢(x,y) ≤ d⁢(x,xi)+d⁢(xi,xl)+d⁢(xl,y)
< ε+max1≤s,t≤n⁡d⁢(xs,xt)+ε=M

∎
