completely Hausdorff


Definition 1.

[1] Let (X,τ) be a topological space. Suppose that for any two different points x,y∈X,x≠y, we can find two disjoint neighborhoods

Ux,Vy∈τ,x∈Ux,y∈Yy

such that their closures are also disjoint:

Ux¯∩Vy¯=∅.

Then we say that (X,τ) is a completely Hausdorff space or a T2⁤12 space.

Notes

A synonym for functionally Hausdorff space is Urysohn space [1]. Unfortunately, the definition of completely Hausdorff and T2⁤12 are not as standard as one would like since. For example, the term completely Hausdorff space is also used to mean a functionally Hausdorff space (e.g. [2]). Nevertheless, in the present convention, we have the implication:

functionally Hausdorff⇒completely Hausdorff⇒T2=Hausdorff,

which suggests why the T2⁤12 name have been used to denote both completely Hausdorff spaces and functionally Hausdorff spaces.
