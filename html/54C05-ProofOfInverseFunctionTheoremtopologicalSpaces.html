proof of inverse function theorem (topological spaces)


We only have to prove that whenever A⊂X is an open set, then also B=(f-1)-1⁢(A)=f⁢(A)⊂Y is open (f is an open mapping). Equivalently it is enough to prove that B′=Y∖B is closed.

Since f is bijective we have B′=Y∖B=f⁢(X∖A)

As A′=X∖A is closed and since X is compact A′ is compact too (this and the following are well know properties of compact spaces). Moreover being f continuous we know that also B′=f⁢(A′) is compact. Finally since Y is Hausdorff then B′ is closed.
