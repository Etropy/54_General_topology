condensation point


Let X be a topological space and A⊂X. A point x∈X is called a condensation point of A if every open neighbourhood of x contains uncountably many points of A.

For example, if X=ℝ and A any subset, then any accumulation point of A is automatically a condensation point. But if X=ℚ and A any subset, then A does not have any condensation points at all.

We have further classifications of condensation point where the topological space is an ordered field. Namely,

  1. 1.

    unilateral condensation point: x is a condensation point of A and there is a positive ϵ with either (x-ϵ,x)∩A countable or (x,x+ϵ)∩A countable.

  2. 2.

    bilateral condensation point: For all ϵ>0, we have both (x-ϵ,x)∩A and (x,x+ϵ)∩A uncountable.

    If κ is any cardinal (i.e. an ordinal which is the least among all ordinals of the same cardinality as itself), then a κ-condensation point can be defined similarly.
