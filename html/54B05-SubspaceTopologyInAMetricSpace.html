subspace topology in a metric space


Theorem 1.

Suppose X is a topological space whose topology is induced by a metric d, and suppose Y⊆X is a subset. Then the subspace topology in Y is the same as the metric topology when by d restricted to Y.

Let d′:Y:Y→ℝ be the restriction of d to Y, and let

Br⁢(x) = {z∈X:d′(z,x)<r},
Br′⁢(x) = {z∈Y:d′(z,x)<r}.

The proof rests on the identity

Br′⁢(x)=Y∩Br⁢(x),x∈Y,r>0.

Suppose A⊆Y is open in the subspace topology of Y, then A=Y∩V for some open V⊆X. Since V is open in X,

V=∪{Bri⁢(xi):i=1,2,…}

for some ri>0, xi∈X, and

A = ∪{Y∩Bri⁢(xi):i=1,2,…}
= ∪{Bri′⁢(xi):i=1,2,…}.

Thus A is open also in the metric topology of d′. The converse direction is proven similarly.
