subbasis


Let (X,𝒯) be a topological space. A subset 𝒜⊆𝒯 is said to be a subbasis if the collection ℬ of intersections of finitely many elements of 𝒜 is a basis (http://planetmath.org/BasisTopologicalSpace) for 𝒯.

Conversely, given an arbitrary collection 𝒜 of subsets of X, a topology can be formed by first taking the collection ℬ of finite intersections of members of 𝒜 and then taking the topology 𝒯 generated by ℬ as basis. 𝒯 will then be the smallest topology such that 𝒜⊆𝒯.
