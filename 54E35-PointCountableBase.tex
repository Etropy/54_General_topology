Let $X$ be a topological space.  A basis $\mc{B}$ of $X$ is a \emph{point countable base} if every point of $X$ is contained in at most countably many sets of $\mc{B}$.

Any uniform base is a point countable base, and a theorem of R. W. Heath states that every semimetric space with a point countable base is developable.
